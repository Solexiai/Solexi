General

What Solexi.ai is, what it’s for, and what it is not.

What is Solexi.ai?
⌄

Solexi.ai is designed to be a digital legacy platform: a structured place to store important digital materials (memories, documents, instructions), and to define controlled access for trusted people over time.

  • Vault: organize files and narratives in a private structure.
  • People & permissions: define who can access what and when.
  • Delivery: future messages or instructions when conditions are met.
How does it work (simple overview)?
⌄
  1. Create your private vault (folders, categories, notes).
  2. Add content (photos, videos, documents, letters, instructions).
  3. Map people (family, executor, trusted contacts) and permissions.
  4. Set rules (dates, milestones, verified events, inactivity windows).
  5. Review & export so you keep control and portability.

If your product roadmap includes AI modules, they should be optional, transparent, and permissioned—never “magic” access.

Is Solexi.ai the same as genealogy sites (Ancestry / FamilySearch)?
⌄

Not the same. Genealogy sites specialize in discovering historical records. Solexi.ai focuses on organizing modern digital legacy and controlling private access.

  • Genealogy: records, archives, historical matching.
  • Solexi: private vault, permissions, future delivery, governance logic.
Who is Solexi.ai for?
⌄
  • Individuals: keep important materials structured and private.
  • Families: reduce confusion, centralize “what matters”.
  • Creators: preserve work, context, and instructions.
  • Institutions: maintain controlled access and continuity policies.

Security & Privacy

Trust is architecture: encryption, authentication, permissions, auditing, and clear documentation.

Is my data secure?
⌄

A trustworthy legacy system should document security controls in plain language. Do not rely on marketing claims. Publish your real stack: encryption in transit, encryption at rest, authentication options, and access logging.

  • Encryption in transit: TLS/HTTPS for network protection.
  • Encryption at rest: encrypted storage for vault content.
  • Strong auth: MFA support and secure recovery flows.
  • Least privilege: permissions scoped by person + content + time.
  • Auditability: access logs and change history.

Can Solexi.ai staff see my private files?
⌄

This depends on your chosen architecture. If you implement a zero-knowledge model, the service cannot read user content without user-held keys. If you implement standard cloud encryption, the service may still be able to access content in specific support or legal scenarios.

The correct answer must match what you actually ship. Publish a short “Who can access what?” matrix on your Security page.

What’s your privacy approach and data retention policy?
⌄

A modern product should state: what data is collected, why it’s collected, how long it’s retained, how deletion works, and how exports work.

  • Collection: account info, uploaded content, security logs.
  • Purpose: delivering the service, preventing abuse, improving reliability.
  • Retention: defined timelines and what remains in backups.
  • Deletion: what “delete” means (immediate vs delayed vs backups).

Heir Access

Controlled access means rules, verification, and audit trails—configured by you.

How do heirs or trusted contacts access my vault?
⌄

You define who can access what, when, and how. Typical triggers include:

  • Date-based: “release on YYYY-MM-DD”.
  • Milestone: “release when they turn 18”.
  • Verified event: “release after verified passing”.
  • Inactivity: “release after X months without login.”

If you use document-based verification, define the exact process publicly (what’s required, who reviews, average SLA).

Can heirs delete or modify my original files?
⌄

Best practice is granular permission levels: view-only, download, contribute, or administer. Many legacy systems prefer preservation-first defaults (view/download) unless you explicitly grant edit rights.

Your product should clearly state default permissions and what happens on disputes.

Can I include an executor or lawyer in the access plan?
⌄

Yes—if your system supports “trusted roles”, you can grant limited access to an executor, lawyer, or designated contact for verification and controlled release.

For legal-grade workflows, publish a “role permissions” table and an audit log model.

AI Features

“AI” should be useful, permissioned, and explainable—never vague or creepy.

Do you offer AI avatars or voice features?
⌄

If enabled, AI features should work like this: you provide content (stories, notes, recordings), and the system can help you organize, search, and summarize your vault, or create optional conversational experiences.

Any voice/avatar feature must include: explicit consent, clear training data scope, and protections against misuse (for example: access control, watermarking, or restricted generation).

Is my content used to train public AI models?
⌄

This must be a clear yes/no in your policy. If you ever allow training, it should be opt-in, scoped, and documented. The default expectation for sensitive legacy content is no public training unless users explicitly choose otherwise.

Publish the real answer on Privacy Policy and AI Policy.

What does “AI search” actually mean here?
⌄

Practical definition: better retrieval. Example capabilities: semantic search (“find the story about the old cabin”), entity tagging (people/places), and summaries that link back to original sources.

If you use embeddings or vector search, document where embeddings are stored and how they are protected.

Export & Portability

You should always be able to leave with your data.

Can I export my data?
⌄

A credible legacy platform should provide export paths such as: a ZIP archive of uploaded files, a human-readable PDF summary, and structured exports for metadata.

Publish supported formats and limitations on Export docs.

What happens if the company changes (acquisition) or shuts down?
⌄

This is a critical trust question. Good answers include: guaranteed export windows, documented continuity plans, and clear retention timelines.

If you claim escrow, trusts, or continuity mechanisms, document them publicly and precisely.

Legal & Compliance

Is Solexi.ai providing legal advice?
⌄

No—software can help you organize information and express instructions, but legal advice requires a qualified professional. Your site should include a clear disclaimer and a recommendation to consult counsel for estate planning.
